Elephants

As the world's largest land mammal, elephants have quite the commanding presence. But did you know elephants can't jump? Or that baby elephants lose their first set of teeth and tusks, just like humans? Learn about both Asian and African elephants and the threats facing these highly intelligent animals today.

Size

Elephants are about 10 feet tall at the shoulder and can weight up to 18000 lbs. The largest elephant ever was about 13 feet tall and weight about 24,000 lbs.

Intelligence

Elephants can recognize themselves in the mirror. They can also problem solve and use tools. Their most profound intelligence comes with their ability to remember.

What they eat

On the daily, elephants eat about 220 lbs of food, and drink about 50 gallons every day.

Activity

The size of the elephant hinders its ability to move. It can run and swim, but elephants are one of the only animals who cannot jump. Plus, because they stand on their toes, they have no spring in their legs.

300 lbs of Muscle: The trunk

They can pick up objects over twice the size as their trunk; However, the small flap on the tip of their trunk is sensitive enough to feel and pick up small berries.

Baby Elephants loose their Teeth

Weighing in at 50 lbs. and growing as long as 6 feet, elephant tusks are a type of tooth. Baby elephants loose their first set of tusks at about 1 year old. They begin to grow their adult tusks at this point.

Vulnerable Mammals

Habitat fragmentation, poaching for ivory, and conflict with farmers are the main reasons populations have declined. While the African elephant is not engangered, it is a vulnerable species. The Asian elephant is endangered.

Conserve

By paying attention and supporting conservation efforts, you can help play an active role in keeping elephants on earth for good.

Learn

Study the intelligence of these great beasts, and find out how they communicate with eachother, and interact with their habitats.

Act

Find ways to interact with them: support artists who photograph, paint, and sculpt. Go on safari or visit them at your local zoo.
